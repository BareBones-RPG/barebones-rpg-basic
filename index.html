The Role-Playing Game

BAREBONES BASIC ( B B B X )

Roll Low. Play Simple.

A system-agnostic, setting-neutral RPG that strips everything back to d6 and lets the story breathe life.

Core Mechanic

How to Play

Each Player Character (PC) has a set of attributes, each a number from 1 to 5. To perform an action tied to an attribute, roll a single d6. Roll equal to or lower than the attribute's value and you succeed.

The Core Roll

1
Choose the relevant attribute (1–5)
2
Roll 1d6
3
Roll ≤ attribute = SUCCESS
4
Roll > attribute = FAILURE

Critical (1): Double effect. Best possible outcome. Fumble (6): Something goes terribly wrong.

Gameplay Example

Referee "You're in a small room — 15×15 feet. A single wooden door ahead. It's stuck."
Player (Fighter) "I want to listen at the door before I open it."
Referee "Roll an INSIGHT test." [Player rolls 4 — INSIGHT is 2. Failure.] "You hear faint shuffling but can't be sure."
Player "Fine. I force the door open."
Referee "Roll MIGHT." [Player rolls 1 — a Critical!] "You throw it open with ease — and catch the Goblins completely by surprise!"

Boost & Burden

Helpers, gear, and circumstances can shift the odds. A Boost adds +1 to your effective attribute; a Burden subtracts -1. Maximum chance is always 5-in-6; minimum is always 1-in-6.

Boost (+1)

Using proper gear for the task, or getting help from another PC. Maximum 2 Boosts on one roll, capped at 5.

Burden (-1)

Improvising with wrong tools, prone, distracted. Maximum 2 Burdens on one roll, floored at 1.

Character Stats

Attributes

Every character has five attributes rated 1–5. At character creation, the Referee rolls 3d3 or 4d3; that total is your point pool to distribute freely. No attribute can start at 0 or exceed 5.

AGILITY Agility Defend · Balance · Swim · Flee
INSIGHT Insight Perceive · Aim · Charm · Recall
MIGHT Might Crush · Lift · Hit · Grapple · Endure
WILLPOWER Willpower Resist fear · Cast spells · Rituals
LUCK Luck When fate fails you, Luck is your edge.

Using Luck

Luck can be spent before or after a roll. Spend carefully — it only returns through bold play.

+1 or +2 Boost Spend before rolling. Cannot exceed 5-in-6.
Act First Go first in initiative regardless of the roll. Declare at round start.
Bend Fate Neutralize a fumble. It simply doesn't happen.
Feat of Fate Attempt something normally impossible. The referee decides if fate allows it.
Regaining Luck: Roll a 6 on any test (failure becomes opportunity), or the Referee awards it for bold, risky, or cinematic roleplay moments. Cannot exceed 5.

Health & Damage

Understanding Hits

BBBX replaces traditional Hit Points with Hits. Every character starts with 3 Hits. When you take damage, roll your Armor Die — if the result is higher than the incoming Hits, the armor absorbs it.

3
Starting Hits
>
Armor Die must EXCEED incoming damage
≤
Armor fails — mark the Hits
Optional: Exploding Die. Roll a 1 on any attack? Roll again — if that's also a 1, the critical explodes for an extra +1 Hit of damage.

At 0 Hits

Your PC is still standing — battered, exhausted, desperate. Now each hit goes straight to an attribute:

  • Combat dmgMIGHT −1
  • Trap/HazardAGILITY −1
  • Knowledge failINSIGHT −1
  • SupernaturalWILLPOWER −1

0 Attribute Consequences

  • MIGHT 0Dead. No saving throw. Make a new character.
  • AGILITY 0Dazed. Cannot move. Needs a breather to regain 1 point.
  • INSIGHT 0Disheartened. Refuses to continue. Another PC can attempt an INS test to snap them out.
  • WILLPOWER 0Catatonic. 3 days rest; then must roll a 1 on WIL test to recover. Failure means another 3 days — a second failure ends the character.
Rules of Engagement

Combat Phase

Combat is fast, deadly, and chaotic. Initiative is rolled every round — roll 1d6 for the party; 1–3 means PCs go first, 4–6 means enemies go first.

1

Roll Initiative (every round)

One player rolls 1d6 for the party. 1–3: PCs first. 4–6: enemies first. Combat is a tug-of-war — no predictable order.

2

Player Turn

Attack (MIG for melee, INS for ranged), cast a spell (INS or WIL), move, use gear, or perform a creative action. On a hit, weapon damage is automatic.

3

Enemy Turn & Defense

PCs always roll to defend. Choose: Dodge (AGI test) or Parry/Block (MIG test — success reduces weapon tier instead of armor tier).

4

Damage & Armor

On a hit, roll Armor Die. Result must EXCEED incoming damage or the PC marks the Hits. Then start a new round.

Critical & Fumble

1 Critical

Attack: Double damage. Foe's armor drops one tier.

Defense: Evade with no armor roll required. Get a free counter-attack if the enemy is Near.

6 Fumble

Attack: Drop weapon 1d6 feet away (1–3) or weapon loses a tier (4–6). Ranged: weapon breaks.

Defense: Suffer a Critical Hit (2 Hits). Armor drops one tier.
