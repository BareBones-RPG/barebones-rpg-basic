Character Creation...

The Game Master (Referee) rolls 3d3. The result is the total number of points a player may distribute across their four attributes:

  • Agility — Defend, flee, climb, sneak
  • Insight — Perceive, investigate, recall knowledge, use magic
  • Might — Lift, strike, smash, grapple
  • Willpower — Resist fear, cast spells, activate relics
  • Luck — Luck is your edge when fate fails you. Roll a 6? Gain 1 Luck. Spend 1 to re-roll, ignore a critical fail, or do max (2) strike.

Each attribute must be between 1 and 5. You cannot set an attribute to 0 or exceed 5.

Optional Rule: Heroic or Gritty Builds

Instead of rolling, the Referee may assign a flat value:

  • 8 points — Gritty, low-power characters
  • 10 points — Heroic, high-power characters

Step 2: Choose an Archetype

Archetypes provide thematic direction. They suggest a key attribute but are not mechanical classes:

  • Divine (Willpower): Clerics, druids, spiritual warriors
  • Fighter (Might): Barbarians, duelists, bruisers
  • Magic-User (Insight): Wizards, scholars, seers
  • Rogue (Agility): Thieves, scouts, swashbucklers

Step 3: Select a Kin

Your Kin determines species/ancestry and grants a small bonus and a once-per-session ability:

  • Halfling: +1 Agility, +2 Luck; ignore one failed roll per session
  • Human: +1 to any stat, +1 Luck; once per session, justify using a different attribute
  • Dwarf: +1 Might, +1 luck; re-roll one poison-related Might test
  • Elf: +1 Agility or Insight, +1 luck; re-roll one Insight recall test
  • Goblin: +1 Agility, +2 luck; re-roll one failed trap test
  • Half-Orc: +1 Might, +1 luck; re-roll one intimidation-related Insight test

Step 4: Strikes

Each character begins with 3 Strikes. These replace HP. When you take damage and the maximum is rolled on a damage die, you lose a Strike.
