Character Creation

Step 1: Assign Attribute Points

Heroic or Gritty Builds

Instead of rolling points, the Game Master (Referee) may assign a flat number of points:

Gritty Vs Heroic Points
  • 8 points — Gritty, low-powered characters
  • 10 points — Heroic, high-powered characters

Each attribute must be between 1 and 5. You cannot set an attribute to 0 or exceed 5.

Optional Rule: Game Master Rolls for Points

The referee rolls 4d3. The total result is the number of points the player may distribute across the five attributes:

  • Agility — Defend, flee, climb, sneak
  • Insight — Perceive, investigate, recall knowledge, how many spells one can remember
  • Might — Lift, strike, smash, grapple
  • Willpower — Resist fear, cast spells, activate relics
  • Luck — Your edge when fate fails. Roll a 6 on any test? Gain 1 Luck. Spend 1 to re-roll a die, ignore a critical fail, or deal maximum damage (2 Strikes).

Each attribute must be between 1 and 5. You cannot set an attribute to 0 or exceed 5.

Step 2: Choose an Archetype

Archetypes provide thematic direction. They suggest a key attribute but are not mechanical classes:

  • Divine (Willpower/Insight): Clerics, druids, spiritual warriors
  • Fighter (Might/Agility): Barbarians, duelists, bruisers
  • Magic-User (Insight/Willpower): Wizards, scholars, seers
  • Rogue (Agility/Insight): Thieves, scouts, swashbucklers

Step 3: Pick a Kin

Kin represents the species or ancestry of your PC. It grants a small stat bonus and a once-per-session ability:

  • Halfling: +1 Agility, +2 Luck; ignore one failed roll per session and turn it to a successful roll
  • Human: +1 to any stat, +1 Luck; once per session, justify using a different attribute for a roll
  • Dwarf: +1 Might, +1 Luck; re-roll one poison-related Might test
  • Elf: +1 Agility or Insight, +1 Luck; re-roll one Insight-based recalling history test
  • Goblin: +1 Agility, +2 Luck; re-roll one failed trap-related test
  • Half-Orc: +1 Might, +1 Luck; re-roll one intimidation-related Insight test

Step 4: Starting Strikes

Characters begin with 3 Strikes, which replace traditional HP. When you take damage and the attacker rolls the maximum value on their damage die, you lose 1 Strike.
