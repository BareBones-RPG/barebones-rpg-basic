Rolling the Dice

Each Player Character (PC) has a set of attributes, each rated from 1 to 5. To perform an action tied to an attribute, roll a six-sided die (1d6).

If the roll is equal to or lower than the attribute's score, the action is successful. If it’s higher, the action fails. This includes rolling for combat, or doing any test that is connected to an attribute.

Understanding Attribute Rolls

Each attribute is written as a chance—like 3/6 or 3-in-6—meaning the player succeeds on a roll of 3 or less.

Criticals and Fumbles

  • 1 on a d6 is a critical success.
  • 6 on a d6 is a fumble (critical failure).

Combat Critical Success

  • Melee or Missile Attack: Automatically deals Strike damage.
  • Defense: Avoid the attack and gain a free counterattack (deal damage without rolling to hit).

Combat Fumble

  • Melee or Missile Attack: Your weapon breaks.
  • Defense: Take double damage and reduce armor by one tier (d6 → d4 → d2 → 0).

Using Luck

Luck is a special attribute representing a character’s ability to twist fate. Players decide how many points to assign to it during character creation. Luck can be used in the following ways:

  • Gain a +2 bonus to a test (before rolling). This improves your chance but can’t exceed a 5-in-6 chance.
  • Cancel a fumble (after rolling a 6), turning it into a normal failure.
  • Automatically deal a Strike when your attack hits (skip the damage roll).
  • Act first in initiative for the round, regardless of your roll.

Note: Once spent, Luck is gone until regained. The Referee may allow Luck to refresh when a player rolls a 6 during a test or through roleplay-worthy moments.
